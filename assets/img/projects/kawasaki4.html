Recent Updates of Ninja 250

The 2023 Kawasaki Ninja 250 is now available in the Malaysian market, and it includes the exclusive Ninja 250 Ohlins Edition. This special edition, priced at RM20,500 (excluding road tax, insurance, and registration), is limited to just 180 units.

One of the key features of the Ninja 250 Ohlins Edition is the inclusion of an Ohlins KA 744 monoshock from the STX 36 Supersport monoshock range as the rear suspension. The front forks remain non-adjustable telescopic units, while the STX 36 monoshock is adjustable for compression, rebound, and height.

Kawasaki Ninja 250 Prices: Kawasaki recently introduced a limited-edition Ninja 250 Ohlins Edition in the Malaysian market that is made available at RM 20,500 (tax excluded). The Ohlins Edition will be limited to 180 units. As of now, the Ninja 250 is made available in the Standard variant (priced at RM 18900) and the ABS variant (priced at RM 20,500).

Kawasaki Ninja 250 Features: The Ninja 250 takes styling cues from its elder sibling, the Ninja H2, and features a fully-faired design that is accentuated by the twin LED headlamps at the front. The suspension unit includes telescopic front forks with 130mm travel and a rear monoshock with 132 mm travel. The Ninja 250 gets a digital-analog instrument cluster.

Kawasaki Ninja 250 Engine: Powering the Ninja 250 is a 249cc parallel-twin, DOHC fuel-injected engine that makes 37 hp of power at 12500 rpm and 23 Nm of torque at 10000 rpm. The engine comes paired with a six-speed gearbox using an assisted slipper-type clutch and chain final drive. The Ninja 250 gets a 14-litre fuel tank. 

Kawasaki Ninja 250 Safety: Braking duties are carried out by a 310mm disc brake on the front and a 220 mm disc on the rear, both clamped by dual-piston calipers for sharper feedback. The Ninja 250 comes equipped with Anti-Lock Braking System for additional safety.

Kawasaki Ninja 250 Rivals: As of now, the direct rivals of the Kawasaki Ninja 250 in the Malaysian market are the Yamaha YZF R-15 (priced at RM 22,998) and the Honda CBR250RR.

Kawasaki Ninja 250 Review 2024

Abhishek Katariya says

Two years back, the Japanese bike maker Kawasaki added Ninja 250 in its Malaysian lineup, which replaced the earlier Ninja 250 and the 250SL. The superbike bears a higher craze among the enthusiasts as it brings some exciting upgrades over the models it replaced. Belonging to the highly-competitive bike segment, Baby Ninja could be the first choice of those who are getting on their first real motorcycle. Kawasaki is also planning to assemble motorcycles locally through Modenas. And, the very first model to come out of this deal would be Modenas Ninja 250 SE ABS. It is expected to launch somewhere around the second quarter of this year. The price of this bike is still not confirmed; however, the current Kawasaki Ninja 250 is priced at RM 23,071.

Kawasaki Ninja 250 Design

The Ninja series from Kawasaki has always been a head-turner, and so is this baby, the Ninja 250. For this, Yamaha has taken styling cues from the flagship Ninja H2 and paid great attention to its large bodywork and detailing. The fit and finishing of the superbike are top class.

The sharp front section features the chiseled full fairing, and the twin LED headlamps. The Ninja 250’s instrumental cluster is the same as the one that we see on the Ninja 650 and imparts a very racy vibe. The analog-digital console has a very tidy approach. The large dial for the tachometer will surely let the rider to rev the engine higher on the open roads.

Further, the fuel tank is well contoured, especially in the place where the rider locks his knees. This makes for better handling and comfort.

Ninja 250 Ride & Handling

Without any doubt, Ninja 250 is a great performer on the road. The increase in power delivery has seen the fun-to-ride parameter surge higher up. The updated engine is much more responsive than it used to be. The mechanical numbers come at a very high rev range, something that will thrill the riders who keep a tap on the tachometer while riding.

Although 795 mm seat height is not the highest in the segment, it is good enough for all riders. The overall curb weight of 164 kgs is also quite manageable. The bike has an abundant amount of torque available at lower revs, which makes the ride equally smooth while commuting in the city.

Another exciting feature of baby Ninja is the radiator fan cover, a setup that directs the engine heat away from the rider’s body parts. This significantly reduces the rider's discomfort level while idling in a halted traffic and keeps the engine bay cooler. 

Kawasaki Ninja 250 Engine & Fuel Consumption

The latest Kawasaki Ninja 250 has seen the engine reworked, and as a result, it makes more power in comparison to the model it replaced. The 249-cc powertrain comes with liquid-cooling and four valves per cylinder capable of generating 39 PS of maximum power at a whopping 12,500 rpm and 23.5 Nm of peak torque at 10,000 rpm. The produced power is transferred to the wheels through a 6-speed gearbox.

 

Ninja 250 Braking & Safety

The Baby Ninja has a great stopping power too. The force comes from a 310 mm disc brake up front while the rear wheel has a 220 mm unit. Both the sides have dual-piston calipers for sharper feedback. Besides this, the thick and sticky wheels help to keep the Ninja firmly planted on the turf, even when you try to test its limits. The sturdy wheels would be able to prevent a mishap from skidding in all the situations, be it hard braking or leaning at a right angle around a corner.

 

Kawasaki Ninja 250 Verdict

Kawasaki Ninja 250 is one of the most potent fully-faired sports motorcycles available in the Malaysian market. If you are looking for a good starter bike to begin a lifetime of riding, it is not only prettier and faster but also a much better all-rounder than before. It really manages to keep up the name of the Ninja series, though being a little on the expensive side.
